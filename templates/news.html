SpearHeading Skill development across India

The "SpearHeading Skill Development across India" scheme aims to boost job creation and skill development in textiles and related sectors. It encourages industry participation, skill enhancement in traditional sectors, and livelihood generation across society. The scheme targets training 10 lakh individuals over 3 years (2017-20) with a budget of 1300 crore. Participation is open to Textiles Industry/Associations registered under Central or State Government/Chambers of Commerce.


Encouragement to Research and Innovation in Textile Sector

The scheme encourages research and innovation in the textile sector to boost ease of doing business and support "Make in India" with zero defects in manufacturing. It aims to enhance investment, productivity, quality, and employment, promoting exports and reducing imports in the textile industry. Additionally, it indirectly stimulates investment in benchmarked textile machinery manufacturing

Integrated Wool Development Programme

The primary goal of the scheme is to stop the decline of the wool production in India and uplift the manufacturing process by offering various components in this scheme and to enable a steady growth in the production of wool among all rural wool-producing sectors.


Production Linked Incentive Scheme (PLI)

offers a production linked incentive to boost domestic manufacturing and attract large investments in mobile phone manufacturing and specified electronic components, including Assembly, Testing, Marking and Packaging (ATMP) units.


PM MITRA scheme

The scheme will develop integrated large scale and modern industrial infrastructure facility for total value-chain of the textile industry for example, spinning, weaving, processing, garmenting, textile manufacturing, processing & printing machinery industry.


Scheme for Integrated Textile Park (SITP)

The Government's SITP initiative develops cutting-edge textile parks, with 54 such parks sanctioned. It has drawn an investment of Rs. 14,243.69 crore and generated employment for 1,05,709 individuals, fostering economic growth.


The Integrated Processing Development Scheme (IPDS)

The Integrated Processing Development Scheme (IPDS) addresses textile processing industry challenges, including water scarcity and environmental pollution. It focuses on developing parks with shared facilities like effluent treatment plants and water supply systems, benefiting small and medium enterprises.
